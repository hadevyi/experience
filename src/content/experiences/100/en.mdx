eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The SSAFYnity Potluck Party was an alumni social event where participants brought small dishes they wanted to introduce, shared them, and connected through games.
    It was held offline on September 27, 2025, at Plants in Partyroom & BBQ Sillim.
  background: |
    As a regular SSAFYnity event, it was prepared to create a casual space where alumni members could meet and talk comfortably.
    The capacity was 50 people, with 35 registrants and 37 total participants.
  role: |
    I supported event check-in and helped the event continue with light facilitation and natural networking.
    Rather than structuring the event around large program segments, I focused on creating an atmosphere where participants could talk comfortably.
  operation: |
    I prepared supplies at each necessary location so participants and operators could use them naturally during the event.
    Since the purpose was casual exchange, I reduced complex programming and focused on necessary guidance and on-site support.
  collaboration: |
    I minimized the operational burden so organizers did not need to intervene heavily throughout the event.
    I maintained a flow where participants could share food and talk on their own.
  challenge: |
    The venue felt quite crowded compared to the number of participants.
    Instead of keeping the prepared supplies in fixed places, I rearranged them on the spot according to the on-site flow.
  result: |
    After the event, some participants continued communicating with one another, showing that the exchange carried through.
    The organizers were also able to enjoy the event while operating it comfortably, and the participants responded positively.
  learning: |
    For a social event, I confirmed that creating an environment where participants can move and talk naturally can matter more than adding many structured programs.
    I also learned that space density and supply placement need to be adjusted flexibly according to the on-site situation.
