  context: |
    Before this, I had worked at several shops near school, but the work was physically demanding and difficult to balance during exam periods.
    Senior students in my department introduced me to the national work-study program, and I applied because it offered a better environment and hours that could fit around my classes.

    The Korea Student Aid Foundation first confirmed eligibility based on income criteria, then I applied to campus offices and was selected through interviews.

    The details differed by workplace, but the common responsibilities included operational support, phone inquiries, research tasks, and administrative assistance.
    Workplace-specific responsibilities and reflections are organized as separate linked experiences.
  collaboration: |
    I worked with staff members across campus offices and supported tasks according to each office's workflow.
    As a student, this gave me a closer look at how different parts of the university operated.
  outcome: |
    Being able to work between 9 a.m. and 6 p.m. around my classes made it much easier to balance work and study.
    The work was less physically demanding than off-campus part-time jobs, and the financial stability helped me save time and energy.
    After work, I could focus more on personal study and lab life, and some workplaces also gave me chances to observe lectures and campus activities more closely.
