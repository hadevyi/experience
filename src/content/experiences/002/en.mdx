affiliation:
  overview: |
    The Department of Computer Engineering at Inje University belonged to the College of Engineering and moved under the College of Software in 2018.
    During my time there, there were detailed major tracks such as computer engineering and information security, along with paths like teacher training, a KAIST track, and the ABEEK engineering education accreditation program.
    Academic clubs were also active enough that joining one felt almost natural for every student.
  reason: |
    I wanted my interest in computers to become more than a personal curiosity and turn into something I could study and build with.
    At the same time, I was not fully certain about my future path yet, so I was also drawn to a major that could keep multiple options open.
    I chose the Department of Computer Engineering because it allowed me to learn programming, data, and systems from the fundamentals.
  startingContext: |
    When I entered the department, I had almost no programming experience, not even having printed "Hello World" before.
    The first programming language I learned was C#, and I had to learn the basic concepts of programming step by step through classes and practice sessions.
  challenges: |
    At first, even basic concepts like data types were difficult to understand, and practice sessions often felt overwhelming.
    I remember staying up all night in the club room because I did not want to move on without understanding what I had missed.
  gains: |
    Most importantly, I gained the feeling that effort could lead to growth.

    The seniors and activities I met there helped me continue the discipline, and the experience became the foundation for the development career that followed.
