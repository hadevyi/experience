eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The SSAFYnity Career Party was a career networking event where participants connected around job roles, domains, and hobbies.
    It was held offline on December 6, 2025, at Multicampus Yeoksam 18F.
  background: |
    It was a large regular event representing SSAFYnity, open to anyone regardless of alumni status.
    The event was recommended for job seekers and junior developers with up to five years of experience, with a capacity of 100 people.
    There were 82 registrants and 91 total participants.
  role: |
    I led the overall event and handled the opening.
    I organized the overall flow so participants could talk naturally within each part.
  operation: |
    I reviewed the number of registrants by the job role, domain, and hobby information they submitted, then used that data to decide part structure and space allocation.
    On the day of the event, I handled space assignment and on-site guidance for each participant group.
  collaboration: |
    I shared the structure of each part so organizers could understand and guide the flow of each space.
    The focus was to help participants easily understand where and with whom they could talk under each topic.
  challenge: |
    Because there were many participants, the hardest part was encouraging people to move to the next space after each part ended.
    Some participants wanted to keep talking in the same place, so I had to balance the event structure with the natural flow of conversation.
  result: |
    Since the event was open to non-alumni as well, people from diverse backgrounds were able to connect.
    After the event, I received feedback that the amount of conversation was especially valuable.
  learning: |
    In a networking event, active participant-led conversation can raise satisfaction, but the operation still needs a structure that helps people move into the next flow.
    Balancing the planned part structure with the natural on-site atmosphere was important.
