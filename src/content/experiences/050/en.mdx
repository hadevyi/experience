community:
  overview: |
    Alrini means "algorithm kid" and was an algorithm study group I created for the SSAFY 5th cohort. It was not limited to a single class and was open to anyone in the cohort.
  motivation: |
    I wanted to create a steady flow for algorithm study within SSAFY, where people could solve problems together and organize concepts they needed.
  role: |
    I created the study group and operated the GitHub organization and problem-solving repository. We communicated mainly through GitHub and Discord, and when members suggested problems they wanted to solve together, I organized them into weekly lists and kept the study running through problem solutions and concept notes.
  contribution: |
    The study covered weekly topics such as algorithm basics, recursion, Stack/Queue, DFS/BFS, Greedy, and preparation for SSAFY algorithm assessments. We met regularly and gathered additionally when needed. Problem solving centered on Baekjoon, Programmers, and SW Expert Academy, and during project periods the study shifted into self-paced solving and mutual Q&A.
  challenges: |
    Because the group was open to the entire cohort, each person needed different problems and moved at a different pace. Instead of forcing one fixed difficulty, I kept the structure flexible so members could suggest problems and continue with people who needed similar practice.
  impact: |
    Beyond problem solving, members who had already received job offers voluntarily shared their experience and gave resume feedback. The group became a community for sharing job preparation and learning information within the SSAFY 5th cohort.
