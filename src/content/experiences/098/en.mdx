    Open Source Contributors is a community that uses AI to lower the entry barrier to open source and helps beginners get started.
    At the same time, it warns against careless AI-generated PRs and emphasizes building a healthier open source ecosystem.
  reason: |
    My interest in open source started when I contributed to a Samsung Network Business project during the SSAFY SSDC course.
    At that time, the contribution happened within given options. Later, I began thinking about how to participate in open source by my own choice.
  startingContext: |
    I participated in the 9th cohort in August 2025 and later joined as an organizer from September 1, 2025.
  challenges: |
    Because open source covers a wide range of projects and practices, I needed time to study and understand the context before participating.
    The difficult part was the amount of time required to judge how I could contribute.
  gains: |
    Beyond contribution guidance, the steady online communication among many participants stood out to me.
    Working alongside people from different experience levels and roles broadened my perspective on open source.
    The strongest lesson was that even when using AI, contribution still needs to be responsible.
