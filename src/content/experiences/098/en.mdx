title: Career Document Mentoring for Senior Industry-Academic Project Students
organization: Inje University SW-Centered University Program
summary: Mentored senior students in an industry-academic project on translating project experience into resumes and portfolios.
oneLine: Mentoring that helped senior students turn project experience into career documents
highlights:
  - "Mentored resume and portfolio organization for two teams"
  - "Reviewed document strengths and gaps based on each student’s experience"
  - "Supported students in defining next action plans for job preparation"
teachingMentoring:
  audience: |
    Senior students participating in an industry-academic project.
    I was responsible for two teams, each consisting of five students.
  reason: |
    The mentoring was intended to help students connect what they had built in the industry-academic project to career documents.
    Since the same project needs to be organized differently in a resume and a portfolio, I helped them clarify each document's purpose and structure.
  content: |
    Covered project organization, resume writing, and portfolio structure.
    Before the sessions, I asked each student to think through their projects and activities.
    Based on that preparation, we reviewed what each person should emphasize and what needed to be added or reorganized.
  approach: |
    The mentoring was conducted once a week for one hour, for a total of four sessions.
    I first helped students bring out individual activities, then reviewed their strengths and gaps and organized how they could improve them in resumes and portfolios.
  response: |
    Students were concerned about whether they needed more activities and how to explain what they already had.
    After the mentoring, I received feedback that they were able to identify their strengths and plan how to fill the missing parts.
  learning: |
    This confirmed that resume and portfolio mentoring is not only about telling students to gain more activities, but also about helping them interpret existing experience accurately and reorganize it for each document's purpose.
