eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The 1st Night of SSAFYnity was a New Year networking party held on January 14, 2023, at Coffee Lounge 55.
    It ran from 17:00 to 20:00 and brought together SSAFYnity alumni and the new executive team, with 112 total participants.
  background: |
    The event was prepared to open the 2nd executive term and create an offline moment for alumni to meet and connect.
  role: |
    I participated as the planning director of the 2nd SSAFYnity executive team.
    I led the overall event planning, including networking programs, on-site flow, and participant interaction design.
    On the event day, I served as the main MC and led the flow from guest introductions and toasts to games and open networking.
  operation: |
    The event was structured around networking, including food, table-based conversations, a photo zone, talent sharing, games, New Year goal word clouds, and a matching activity.
    We used pre-registration information and on-site guidance to help participants mix naturally.
  collaboration: |
    I worked with the 2nd executive team to coordinate venue setup, registration, on-site guidance, and program flow.
    Before participants entered, we checked the space layout and food preparation to create a stable operating environment.
  challenge: |
    As the first regular event of the 2nd term, it needed to set a baseline for future quarterly events.
    Since more than 100 people and invited guests joined the event, we needed to consider venue density, participant experience, and operational quality.
  result: |
    The event reached full registration and recorded 112 total participants including operators and guests.
    We received feedback that it felt like a proper party after a long time and that the event was operated smoothly.
  learning: |
    It showed that large community networking events need both atmosphere design and clear operational flow.
    I learned that alumni events are less about one-way information delivery and more about creating moments where relationships can form.
