---
title: Career Talk for DASOM at Dongyang Mirae University
organization: DASOM, Department of Computer Engineering, Dongyang Mirae University
summary: Delivered a career talk for DASOM titled “Designing My Career,” helping students think about the company and growth direction that fit them.
oneLine: A professional career talk that helped students define their own career criteria
highlights:
  - Organized developer competencies and company selection criteria
  - Framed job searching as a matter of personal priorities, not only getting hired
  - Led to follow-up counseling about realistic timelines and action plans
teachingMentoring:
  audience: |
    Members of DASOM, a computer engineering major club at Dongyang Mirae University.
    Fewer than about 20 people attended.
  reason: |
    I was directly contacted by DASOM, the computer engineering major club at Dongyang Mirae University, to deliver this professional career talk.
    The talk was intended to help students think beyond simply getting hired and consider what kind of company and growth direction would fit them.
    I also wanted to discuss how choices can differ depending on whether each person treats development only as a job or as part of their identity.
  content: |
    The talk, titled “Designing My Career,” covered how to think about a company that fits oneself and how to build a growth strategy.
    It was structured around developer competencies, personal priorities for choosing a company, job-market strategy, and perspectives on development as a career.
    I connected common concerns such as development skills, major-specific strengths, certifications, communication, and documentation to career preparation.
    The final part covered recommended activities for undergraduates, job seekers, and junior developers, along with the importance of consistently organizing experiences.
  approach: |
    The talk was delivered offline for about 40 minutes.
    Rather than simply listing job-preparation methods, I structured the talk around questions that could help students think about their own criteria and strengths first.
  response: |
    After the talk, I received counseling requests about how much time students should spend thinking for themselves and how to close the gap between their current state and their goals.
    Several students shared similar concerns about what they needed to do next and how much time they should realistically give themselves.
  learning: |
    This reaffirmed that a career talk should not present one fixed answer, but help people build criteria for judging the company and growth direction that fit them.
    I also felt that students needed the ability to distinguish between a realistic timeline, what they needed to do, and what they wanted to do.
