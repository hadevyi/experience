  overview: |
    WowSup was a senior graduation-project mobile app designed to help foreign-language learners study together through chat and community features.
    We treated ranking and chart views for foreign-language expressions and newly coined words as a core concept, alongside flows such as profile, friends/chat, story posts and tag search, translation, and a token store.
  background: |
    This project began as my senior graduation project.
    At the time, I was thinking about my future role and wanted to work not only on implementation but also on user experience and screen flow.

    The project later continued as a campus startup club activity, but this record focuses on the graduation-project development period from December 2018 to March 2019.
  scope: |
    The graduation project had review presentations in March, May, September, and November. Teams that passed an earlier review could skip the later presentations.
    Our team passed the first review, becoming one of only two early-passing teams in the department among more than 12 to 13 teams.

    After the graduation-project phase, we released the app on Google Play and operated it for about four months, but we did not maintain it longer because of operating costs.
  contribution: |

    On the development side, I contributed to Android screen implementation and frontend flow.
    The project direction and presentation flow were coordinated together as a team.

  implementation: |
    The app was structured around bottom navigation with profile, chat, story, help, and translation flows.
    Based on the repository, Retrofit was used to communicate with PHP server APIs, and Firebase Realtime Database was used for chat data flow.

    The app included flows for registration and login, profile, friends/chat, story writing and tag search, ranking/chart views, image upload, translation, and a token store.

  outcome: |
