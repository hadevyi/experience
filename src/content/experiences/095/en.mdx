title: University-Industry Mini Project Mentoring
organization: Inje University SW-Centered University Program
summary: Mentored a five-person team as they revisited a department project they had recently completed. Using the project flow from planning to operation, I helped them identify missing deliverables and improve design and testing documents.
oneLine: Mentoring that helped students reorganize a project through deliverables
highlights:
  - Mentored one five-person team
  - Four weekly one-hour sessions
  - Focused feedback on design and testing documents
teachingMentoring:
  audience: |
    This mentoring was for one five-person team participating in a university-industry mini project program.
    The students had recently completed a department project, and the mentoring used that project as the review target.
  reason: |
    I wanted to help students understand that building a project is not only about implementation, but also about knowing what to leave behind at each stage.
    The goal was to turn an already completed project into a record they could explain through planning, design, development, testing, and operation.
  content: |
    I covered the role of deliverables at each project stage.

    - Problem definition and requirements in the planning stage
    - Structure, screens, and feature flow in the design stage
    - Implementation details and change tracking in the development stage
    - Verification criteria and results in the testing stage
    - Usage, deployment, and maintenance perspectives in the operation stage
  approach: |
    I ran four weekly mentoring sessions, each about one hour long.
    Rather than creating a new project from scratch, the team used their recently completed department project as the case.

    For each stage, I first explained why the document matters, then reviewed what could be added or clarified in their existing project.
  response: |
    When reviewing the team's previous project, I found that design deliverables were often missing or barely documented.
    I emphasized that students preparing for job searches should organize design and testing deliverables so they can explain their project process more clearly.

    After the mentoring, students said the process helped them understand how to organize their own projects.
  learning: |
    I confirmed that even when students have project experience, learning and decision-making context can fade without documentation.
    Revisiting a completed project helped make the need for records more concrete.
    Design and testing deliverables were especially important because they can show the thinking process and verification criteria behind the final result.
