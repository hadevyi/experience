    This was a summer 2019 internship at the Busan Joint Research Office under the Smart ICT Convergence Research Group of the Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute (ETRI).

    In my lab, students usually spent vacation periods either doing external internships or continuing lab-based study. Since this was my last vacation before graduation, I decided to apply. Many senior lab members had also done this internship, and among two team options, I chose the AI-related track.

    The main keyword throughout the internship was interactive media.

    My task was to work on an AI-based video editing flow where foreground and background separation from the first frame of a video could be applied to the rest of the video. The implementation details are documented separately in the connected background removal editor project, while this record focuses on the internship environment and working style.
  collaboration: |
    There were four interns in total, divided into two-person teams.

    I worked with one other intern on the task, learning how to understand the assignment within a research institute setting and study what was needed while applying it directly.
  outcome: |
    This was the first time Python became necessary for my work, so I had to study it and apply it almost immediately. It was difficult, but it was meaningful to learn AI concepts and apply them to a real task.

    Through this experience, I was introduced to research-institute development, practical development flow, and the image processing and video editing domain. Most importantly, this internship helped me decide that I wanted to continue as a developer.
