teachingMentoring:
  audience: |
    The course was made public for anyone aspiring to become an SW developer.
  reason: |
    After my alma mater was selected for the SW-Centered University Program, I was invited to create an open online course.
    It was an opportunity to connect topics I had previously covered through separate special lectures or classes into one series.
  content: |
    I structured the course under the theme “For juniors dreaming of becoming SW developers.”
    The first lecture started with the idea that coding is not everything, and the second covered how to manage code.
    The series then moved through developer time management, systematic troubleshooting, strategic experience building, thinking about what kind of developer to become, and building knowledge efficiently.
  approach: |
    I handled planning, teaching, recording, and presentation material production.
    Since I already had experience with online lecture recording, the filming itself was not difficult; the main focus was to present my concerns and methods as one possible approach rather than a fixed answer.
  response: |
    Content that had previously been shared through separate talks or lectures became available as public material that people could revisit.
  learning: |
    Turning topics I had previously discussed separately into one series helped me clarify the standards I consider important for developer growth and learning.
    I also felt that it was important to offer juniors ways of thinking and possible choices, rather than presenting one fixed answer for becoming a developer.
