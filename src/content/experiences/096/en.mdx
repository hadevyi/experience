eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The 3rd SSAFYnale was a public developer conference held by SSAFYnity at Multicampus Yeoksam 18F on June 6, 2025.
    The official archive describes the event under the theme “Next, at the crossroads of change,” focusing on choice and growth amid change.
  background: |
    SSAFYnale is one of SSAFYnity's representative large-scale regular events.
    It was run as a public event especially recommended for job seekers and junior developers with up to five years of experience.
  role: |
    As SSAFYnity's 4th president, I led overall event operations.
    I organized venue layout, organizer roles, and participant flow so the event could run smoothly.

    On the event day, I gave the opening and also participated as a session speaker.
  operation: |
    The event ran offline from 13:00 to 18:30, with recordings planned for later access.
    Entry and exit were limited during talks, and participants could submit questions to speakers in advance when registering.

    Speakers were recruited through an open call, and the timetable reflected each speaker's preferences where possible.
    After the event, optional small-group gatherings by job category were organized by participants, and a separate communication area was provided outside the main presentation room.
  collaboration: |
    I coordinated organizer roles so each person could handle their assigned responsibilities.
    After speaker recruitment, I checked speaker preferences and adjusted the order to fit the overall event flow.
  challenge: |
    Because the event was held on June 6, a public holiday in Korea, participation was lower than expected.
    I concluded that future events should avoid public holidays when possible.
  result: |
    According to the official archive, the event had a capacity of 100, 59 registrants, and 68 total participants including organizers and related guests.
    It was operated as a public event with regular members, general members, and external participants.
  learning: |
    I confirmed again that conference operations require not only strong content, but also space, roles, and participant flow to work together.
    Date selection also directly affects participation, so it needs to be considered as carefully as the event theme and venue.
