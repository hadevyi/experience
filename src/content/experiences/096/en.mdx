---
title: When I Question My Own Growth
organization: SSAFYnity
summary: Delivered a 3rd SSAFYnale talk on questioning growth at work and reviewing my direction through activities beyond my main job.
oneLine: A talk that connected doubts about growth with the meaning of activities outside work
highlights:
  - Turned doubts from repetitive work life into a conference talk topic
  - Reframed development, mentoring, community operations, and event planning as one growth path
  - Offered practical criteria for people unsure how to start activities outside work
mediaInterview:
  background: |
    While leading operations for the 3rd SSAFYnale as SSAFYnity's 4th president, I also participated as a speaker in a separate session.
    As work life became more repetitive, I began questioning my own growth, and I thought this topic could help others who were facing similar concerns.
    The talk organized personal concerns that could not be explained only through my main job.
  context: |
    The 3rd SSAFYnale was a public developer conference held under the theme “Next, at the crossroads of change.”
    This talk was part of the Growth track, where I shared personal reflections on developer growth and direction.
  topics: |
    The talk started with the question of whether I was doing well, then moved through burnout, deeper immersion, avoidance, and the activities that had continued to broaden year by year.
    I used examples such as work-related development, personal development, learning, mentoring, presentations, community operations, teaching, volunteering, and event planning to explain how I searched for agency.
    The latter part covered how to build a more effective day by understanding limits across energy, ability, and time, then organizing schedules, priorities, and routines.
  perspective: |
    The main perspective was to treat experiences beyond the main job not as simply doing more, but as a process of finding personal agency.
    The talk acknowledged both views of development: as a tool for economic activity and as an identity beyond a job.
    Its conclusion was that growth does not have one fixed answer, and the direction each person chooses can become their own answer.
  impact: |
    The talk gave people who were unsure where, with whom, or how to start a way to first examine their own capacity and balance.
    After the session, I received feedback that the talk was helpful from the perspective of someone who wanted to start activities outside work but did not know where to begin.
    The session focused less on presenting a single answer and more on encouraging people to refine their own criteria.
