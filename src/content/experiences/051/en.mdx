  overview: |
    SOLVER was a knowledge exchange web service where users could post questions they could not easily solve alone, and other users could help through text answers or video consultations. The service was designed around the idea that questioners could find solvers, while solvers could build a track record through their knowledge and experience.
  background: |
    This was a 6-week SSAFY 5th common project. The project expanded through Sub1, Sub2, and Sub3 phases, and I participated as team lead across backend, frontend, AWS EC2, and design.
  scope: |
    The project covered Kakao login, signup, interest fields, available time settings, questions, answers, comments, likes, bookmarks, answer selection, group creation and approval, video consultation requests and responses, notifications, messages, points, and profile statistics.
  contribution:
    - As team lead, I worked across backend, frontend, AWS EC2, and design.

    - I joined team lead meetings and coordinated task distribution and project flow.
    - I implemented the service based on planning outputs such as a one-page service brief, requirements specification, ERD, sequence diagrams, common codes, category rules, and point policies.
  roleDetails:

  implementation:

    - The frontend used Vue 2 and Vuex for screens and state management.
    - Video consultation was implemented through a separated Kurento and WebRTC flow.
    - Deployment and file storage flows considered AWS EC2, Docker, and S3.
