  overview: |
    Motion Pose is an open-source Node-RED project that recognizes body and hand motions and connects the recognition results to other service flows. It used webcam and SmartThings IoT Cam inputs, and tracked motion through MediaPipe BlazePose and Hands.
  background: |
    I was selected as an outstanding SSAFY 5th trainee for SSDC, a program where trainees could work with Samsung Electronics mentors. Among the available tracks, I chose the Wireless Business Division assignment and proposed motion recognition as an additional control method for situations where touch or voice control is limited.
  scope: |
    The team developed nine Node-RED nodes, including monitor, body/hand detect, body/hand register, and body/hand find nodes. We also presented hands/body IoT control flows and a home training flow, distributed the package through npm and the Node-RED Library, and contributed Motion Pose to the SamsungAutomationStudio repository.
  contribution:
    - Led project direction, mentor Q&A, and presentation flow as the team lead.
    - Implemented the Hands Pose Node part, including hand motion detection, registration, and search flows.
    - Prepared questions and progress updates as presentation material to use the limited mentoring time effectively.
    - Represented the team in the npm, Node-RED Library, and SamsungAutomationStudio PR contribution flow.
    - Handled the final presentation and demo.
  roleDetails:
    - Team coordination
    - Hands Pose Node development
    - npm and Node-RED distribution flow
    - SamsungAutomationStudio PR contribution
    - Final presentation
  implementation:
    - Built Node-RED node structures that recognized hand/body motions from webcam and IoT Cam inputs.
    - Visualized recognition results and passed them into Node-RED flow data using 20 hand landmarks and 33 body landmarks.
    - Included register/find flows that compare saved motions and incoming keypoints through vector normalization, Cosine Distance, and Weighted Distance.
    - Added timer, result image, and keypoint table flows so users could check the registration state of captured motions.
    - Packaged the nodes so developers could build scenarios such as Bixby home training and SmartThings IoT control inside Node-RED flows.
  challenges:
    - Most of the stack, including Node-RED, MediaPipe, SmartThings IoT Cam, and npm distribution, was new to me.
    - IoT services and physical device integration were unfamiliar, so understanding the overall flow took time.
    - Needed to translate motion recognition model outputs into Node-RED flow data.
    - Had to align node structure, documentation, licensing, and release quality for contribution to Samsung's open-source repository.
  outcome:
    - Distributed `node-red-contrib-motion-pose` through npm and the Node-RED Library.
    - Published Hands IoT Control, Body IoT Control, and Body Home Training as Node-RED example flows.
    - Motion Pose add/update PRs were merged into SamsungAutomationStudio.
    - Received positive feedback from a Samsung mentor for preparing questions and progress updates before mentoring sessions.
    - Learned the importance of open-source distribution and license organization for the first time.
    - This led to the SSAFY 5th SSDC project award and the SSDC 2021 presentation.
