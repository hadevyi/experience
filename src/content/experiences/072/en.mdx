    I wanted the alumni community started by the 1st executive team to continue beyond a one-time effort.

    I served as planning director in the 2nd executive team.

  contribution: |
    I helped define annual regular events and separated planning criteria into social networking events and IT events.
    I supported schedule coordination, role distribution, and preparation flow so quarterly events could actually be operated.

    I also participated in designing operational foundations for the independent organization to sustain events.
  challenges: |
    The 2nd team needed to build a foundation that could support stable operations for a full year.
    It was important to coordinate schedules among operators with full-time jobs and align collaboration across multiple teams.
  impact: |
    This experience helped SSAFYnity move beyond its first year and settle into a community that could operate quarterly regular events.
    It is especially meaningful because the operating direction established during the 2nd executive term is still reflected in the community today.
    I learned the importance of continuity and operational stability in long-term community operations.
