community:
  overview: |
    This was a campus startup club activity under Inje University's LINC+ Program, continued from the WowSup graduation project.

    The team expanded from the original graduation-project members to include lab members, becoming a six-person developer team. While the Appbug activity in 015 focused on aligning designers and developers around a prototype, this activity was closer to operating an existing service and thinking about usability and sustainability.
  motivation: |
    The program office first suggested the opportunity, and the team members also wanted to continue WowSup beyond the graduation project.

    Rather than ending the project as a school assignment, I saw it as an opportunity to operate it as a real service and experience what needs to be considered after development.
  role: |
    In the startup club, I focused more on operation, business planning, and the flow of the club activity.

    I handled external presentations and communication with the program office, and I participated more actively in business-related lectures and special sessions. I spent more time thinking about how to explain and sustain the service than about implementation itself.
  contribution: |
    As WowSup continued as a startup club activity, I organized the business plan and presentation flow and communicated necessary details with the program office.

    During service operation, we had to think about whether the service was actually usable, which personas we should design around, and why users would continue using it.

    I realized that keeping an app alive requires more than implementation: it also requires an operating structure, cost awareness, and a user-centered reason for the service to remain useful. Outcomes that followed this activity are kept as connected experiences, while this record focuses on the operating questions we worked through.
  challenges: |
    The most difficult parts were user acquisition, operating costs, and the administrative flow of the startup club.

    During the graduation-project phase, implementation and presentation quality were the main concerns. In the startup-club phase, however, attracting real users and sustaining the service became much more important. Since the team consisted only of developers, we had to keep broadening our perspective on usability, personas, and operation.
  impact: |
    I learned that building a service and actually maintaining and operating it are completely different challenges.

    After this experience, I began to look at projects not only in terms of feature completion but also in terms of whether users actually need the service, who it is designed for, and whether it can be operated sustainably.
