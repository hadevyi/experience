eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The 2nd SSAFYnale was SSAFYnity's public developer conference held on June 22, 2024 on the 18th floor of Multicampus Yeoksam.
    The official archive describes it as an event under the theme "the intersection of harmony and growth."
  background: |
    After the 1st SSAFYnale, IT events were expanded externally so alumni members could connect with a broader network.
    Speakers were SSAFYnity alumni members, while listening participation was also opened to external attendees, with higher registration priority for alumni members.
  role: |
    As SSAFYnity's 3rd HR team lead, I mainly handled registration and attendee management.
    My responsibilities included final attendee list organization, participation fee checks, registration approval and cancellation guidance, refund processing, and participant notices.
  operation: |
    The event was held offline, and a later recording access flow was also announced.
    As the attendee scale grew, it became important to organize the list and manage approvals based on registration priority.
    I also organized operational guidance for the participant experience, including entry and exit restrictions during talks, advance question collection, job-based dinners, and a separate communication space.
  collaboration: |
    Event planning and speaker composition were handled by the organizer group together, while the HR team owned registration, attendee lists, participation fees, and guidance flow.
    Since the event became larger as an externally open event, participant information and on-site operation standards needed to be aligned with other operation parts.
  challenge: |
    The biggest challenge was scale.
    With external participants included, registration, cancellations, refunds, and participant guidance became more complex.
    I also had to consider how to manage a large number of people on-site and keep organizers stationed where needed.
  result: |
    According to the official archive, there were 145 registrants and 157 total participants including organizers, related members, and invited guests.
    After opening externally, SSAFYnity's visibility increased, and the separate rest area and speaker conversation corner were received as making the event richer.
  learning: |
    In a public event, not only the content but also hidden operation standards such as registration priority, fees, cancellations, refunds, and on-site placement strongly shape the participant experience.
    I also learned that designing spaces where participants can stay and talk, rather than only listen to talks, is part of the conference experience.
