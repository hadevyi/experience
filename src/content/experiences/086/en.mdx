mediaInterview:
  background: |
    I chose this topic because I wanted the conference to include practical concerns that early-career professionals often face, not only technical subjects.
    The talk was framed as personal trial and error, not a universal answer, to the question of how to keep doing many things consistently.
  context: |
    At the time, I was working as a server developer at Bigxdata while continuing community operations, presentations, mentoring, and personal learning.
    The talk drew from the routines and concerns I had actually experienced while handling several roles in parallel.
  topics: |
    The talk started from the experience of understanding personal capacity across energy, ability, and time.
    It then covered calendar planning by year, month, and week; deadline-based to-do management; daily routines around work and rest; and note-taking templates for learning and reflection.
  perspective: |
    The main perspective was not to simply do more, but to continue within a sustainable range.
    I shared how I prioritized the main job while using flexible time and note-taking habits to keep learning and contributing.
  impact: |
    It offered personal examples for students and early-career developers who wanted to review their own time and habits.
    After the session, some attendees reached out with follow-up questions and coffee chat requests, extending the talk into more personal conversations.
