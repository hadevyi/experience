  background: |
    I joined the SSAFY 7th recruitment session as a CS-major panelist after being invited by the SSAFY office. I accepted because I wanted to answer a question I had often heard: "Why would a CS major join another training program?" I also thought my experience could help other CS-major applicants considering SSAFY.
  context: |
    The session was held live and later published on YouTube as an edited video. The panel was divided into CS-major and non-CS-major perspectives, and I participated as the CS-major panelist.
  topics: |
    I explained that even as a computer science major, I had not been fully satisfied with my real service development experience. I had learned CS fundamentals, but I wanted more experience building services, collaborating with others, and completing projects, and SSAFY helped fill that gap.
  perspective: |
    Because it was an official recruitment session, it was important to stay within the publicly shareable range. During the live session, I was careful not to mention confidential or internal operational details, while also organizing my sentences on the spot so applicants could use my experience as a reference.
  impact: |
    It became an official panel experience where I passed my SSAFY 5th trainee experience on to prospective applicants. Later, while working as a practice coach for SSAFY 7th trainees, I heard from trainees that the session had helped them during their application process. It also gave me a chance to publicly organize why I chose SSAFY as a CS major and how I filled my need for real service development experience.
