  overview: |
    This was my first personal technical blog project for recording technical notes.
    I built it as a static blog based on GitHub Pages, Jekyll, and minimal-mistakes.

    The blog was titled "정리가 재밌는 개발자 Blog" and was operated as version 1.0 from July 1, 2020 to July 8, 2021.
  background: |
    I wanted a blog that I could care about and manage myself.
    At the time, I had some resistance toward existing blog platforms because ads or platform UI often interrupted the reading experience, so I wanted to build my own writing space.

    I also needed a personal space to organize what I learned and accumulate technical notes.
  scope: |
    I configured the blog structure using the minimal-mistakes theme, including Korean site settings, author profile, category/tag/year archives, comments, analytics, and search indexing setup.
    I added Disqus comments and Google Analytics for traffic tracking.

    I also managed verification files for Google and Naver search exposure, along with sitemap and feed settings.
  contribution: |
    As a personal project, I handled the blog setup and initial configuration myself.
  roleDetails:
    - Set up a blog with GitHub Pages
    - Customized the minimal-mistakes theme
    - Added Disqus comments
    - Set up Google Analytics
    - Registered the blog for Google/Naver search exposure
    - Configured navigation around categories, tags, and yearly archives
  implementation: |
    I configured site language, title, author profile, social links, comments, analytics, plugins, and default layouts mainly through `_config.yml`.
    In `_data/navigation.yml`, I organized navigation around Category, Tags, and Year.

    The blog used plugins including `jekyll-paginate`, `jekyll-sitemap`, `jekyll-feed`, and `jekyll-include-cache`, and was deployed as a static site through GitHub Pages.
  challenges: |
    Ruby, Bundler, GitHub.io, and Jekyll-based blog operation were all new to me.
    The minimal-mistakes documentation was well organized, but following English documentation and understanding the theme customization flow felt unfamiliar.

    Building and designing the blog was enjoyable, but the amount of setup and maintenance made it harder to write posts frequently.
  outcome: |
    I created a personal space for technical writing and experienced how my records could reach others through traffic analytics.
    It was especially memorable that retrospective posts continued to receive steady traffic.

    However, maintaining the blog began to cost more energy than writing itself, and I wanted to focus more on writing. I decided to move to Velog on July 8, 2021.

    This experience later became a reference point for separating the enjoyment of building a tool from the sustainability of the writing flow.
