eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The 3rd Night of SSAFYnity was a regular offline SSAFYnity event held at Seogyo Place Hongdae on March 8, 2025.
    It ran from 16:00 to 19:00 and centered on a yellow dress code and table networking.
  background: |
    Night of SSAFYnity is one of SSAFYnity's representative large-scale regular events.
    It is held twice a year for alumni members, giving them a place to meet offline and reconnect with the community.

    This event was also the first official event run by SSAFYnity's 4th executive team.
  role: |
    As SSAFYnity's 4th president, I led the overall event and hosted the flow.
    I hosted the official flow from the opening to the alumni association introduction and game segments.

    On site, I handled layout changes, additional supplies, participant support, and organizer role coordination.
  operation: |
    The event included table networking, guest introductions, an awards ceremony, games, a best-dresser selection, and a group photo.
    The main theme color was yellow, and the event used name tags, table arrangements, catering, a photo zone, and games to help participants interact naturally.

    The event capacity was 70, and 62 people registered.
    The final participation record counted 84 people including alumni members, organizers, and related guests.
  collaboration: |
    The event was prepared by SSAFYnity's 4th executive team.
    As president, I coordinated organizer roles and connected on-site support with the decisions needed during the event.
  challenge: |
    Because this was my first event as president, I felt pressure to continue the regular event smoothly under the new administration.
    Managing the participant flow, table setup, games, and networking atmosphere was a key operational point.

    During venue coordination, I had to think carefully about accessibility because some participants were coming from farther locations.

    Since alcohol was served at the event, I also had to keep an eye on the atmosphere and participants' condition so that no one drank too much.
  result: |
    The event was completed successfully without major issues and became SSAFYnity's first official event of 2025.
    It was personally meaningful to complete the first event I led as president in a stable way.
    It also created operating momentum toward the next event, SSAFYnale.
  learning: |
    A large networking event depends not only on program design, but also on seating, guidance, break-time flow, and small devices that help participants start conversations.
    For the first event of a new executive team, the event also needs to build trust for future operations.
    I also learned that venues should be judged by real participant accessibility, and events with alcohol need both an enjoyable atmosphere and a safe environment.
