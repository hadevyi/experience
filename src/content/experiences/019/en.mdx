community:
  overview: |
    At the time, the Department of Computer Engineering had an active academic club culture, to the point where each student was expected to join at least one academic club.
    Since the department had many students, many events and academic presentations were organized around these clubs.
  motivation: |
    I felt that communication around academic activities was somewhat static, and I wanted academic club activities to be more visible.
    That was why I volunteered for the academic operations lead role.
  role: |
    As the academic operations lead, I organized academic club activities, supported freshman promotion, shared club outcomes, and helped operate the year-end academic festival.
    I first focused on understanding and organizing each club's characteristics so that incoming students could understand the options more easily.
  contribution: |
    During freshman entrance season, I organized and promoted the characteristics of each academic club, then continued to share club outcomes throughout the year.
    I created news cards for promotion, and prepared judge guide documents and evaluation sheets for the year-end academic festival.

    The academic festival was run like an exhibition, where each academic club presented its work at a booth and judges evaluated the outcomes.
    Since students could also visit and see popular projects, I had to consider both promotion and on-site operation.
  challenges: |
    Since I only knew my own academic club in detail, understanding the activities of each club was the hardest part.
    I also had to keep thinking about how to introduce different activities under the broader theme of academic work.
  impact: |
    This experience helped me understand different groups, organize their characteristics, and make their activities visible to the right audience.
    It was not only about operating an event, but also about thinking through the meaning and communication of academic activities.
