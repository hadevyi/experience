eventOperation:
  overview: |
    SSAFYnity Job Challenge was created so alumni preparing for job searches could support each other and keep momentum.
    I operated it from cohort 1 in 2024 through cohort 6 in 2025.
  background: |
    Alumni who were still preparing for jobs often felt it was difficult to take part in community activities.
    I designed it as a participation activity focused on positive peer motivation rather than evaluation or screening.
  role: |
    I handled planning, recruitment, communication, and participation checks as a one-person operation.
    Across repeated cohorts, I refined the process for participant onboarding and management.
  operation: |
    I ran each cohort so participants could keep a steady job-search routine.
    For selected active participants, I also coordinated 1:1 feedback opportunities with professionals they wanted to meet.
  collaboration: |
    Although it was run under SSAFYnity, I led the actual planning and operation independently.
    When needed, I coordinated with professionals to create touchpoints between job seekers and working alumni.
  challenge: |
    More people participated than expected, which made manual tracking, participation checks, and communication demanding.
    I had to keep the operating flow consistent while making sure the challenge did not become too burdensome for participants.
  result: |
    Many alumni participated, and some shared that the challenge helped them build better daily habits and eventually find jobs.
    It created a way for job-seeking alumni to stay connected with the community.
  learning: |
    I learned that a community-based job-search activity works best when it helps people keep going together rather than simply checking outcomes.
    Even a small challenge can create behavioral change when operation and feedback connections are designed together.
