community:
  overview: |
    The role was familiar, but the context had changed. In the first year, most students were starting from a similar place. In the second year, returning students joined the group, and questions became more specific because of major-track decisions and more varied classes.
  motivation: |
    After my first-year representative experience, I was recommended for the role again and also volunteered to continue as the second-year representative.
  role: |
    As the second-year representative of the Department of Computer Engineering at Inje University, I shared announcements, responded to student questions, and supported class-wide events and education sessions.
    When the department office organized lectures or special sessions for the whole year, I helped deliver the necessary information and clarified details that students might find confusing.
  contribution: |
    In the first year, I was learning how to adapt to an unfamiliar role. In the second year, I started building my own routine.
    I tried to anticipate questions before announcements were shared, add clarifications in advance, and make the order and wording of each notice easier to follow. As classes and schedules became more diverse, I paid more attention to setting clear communication standards.
  challenges: |
    The hardest part was that the group had become more diverse. Some students were returning from leave, and course choices became more complex. Even within the same year, students needed different information, so I had to put more effort into organizing announcements in a way that everyone could understand.
  impact: |
    I remember when the new first-year representative asked me about how to handle the role and what tips I had.
    It felt rewarding to realize that the way I had worked for a year could become useful to someone else. I also learned that a responsibility done well can be passed forward as a small operating guide for the next person.
    This experience was not only about sharing information well, but also about creating a repeatable way to operate.
    If the first-year representative role was about adapting to something unfamiliar, the second-year representative role was where I began building my own rhythm, standards, and routines.
