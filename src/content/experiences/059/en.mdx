mediaInterview:
  background: |
    Motion Pose won first place in the Wireless Business Division assignment at SSAFY 5th SSDC, and I was selected as a presenter for the SSDC 2021 Share session. The session was an opportunity to share the project's open-source contribution process and use scenarios at Samsung Software Developer Conference, beyond the award result itself.
  context: |
    Due to COVID-19, the conference was held online, and the presentation was recorded separately in advance. Since it was part of Samsung Software Developer Conference, the audience was broader than SSAFY and could include anyone interested in software development, open source, and IoT services.
  topics: |
    I covered the technical implementation of Node-RED motion-recognition nodes, the contribution process to SamsungAutomationStudio, deployment through npm and the Node-RED Library, and real IoT control scenarios. Instead of a live demo, I prepared demo videos and included them in the presentation.
  perspective: |
    I wanted to show that development is not only about building the result, but also about documenting and communicating it so others can understand and use it. I focused on how the project expanded from an internal team output into something that could be shared externally through open-source contribution.
  impact: |
    I presented alone, covering both technical explanation and scenario walkthroughs. Because live demos were difficult, I had to spend additional effort preparing all demo videos in advance. It became a record of extending Motion Pose from a curriculum project into a public conference presentation.
