  overview: |
    PLUS+ was the student council of the Department of Computer Engineering at Inje University.
    I began preparing for the candidacy in November 2018, was elected as president, and represented the department through 2019.
  motivation: |
    After serving as a class representative and participating in student council activities, I wanted to improve the parts of student life that felt inconvenient or inefficient.
    I ran for student council president because I wanted to take responsibility for those issues and handle them more actively.
  role: |
    As president, I was responsible for event planning and operations, budget management, and communication within the department.
    I coordinated decisions by listening to about 18 student council members, class representatives, and department members.
  contribution: |
    I focused on reducing unnecessary spending and increasing cultural activities that students could actually enjoy.
    For example, I removed ceremonial costs from the MT budget and improved prize quality. Instead of continuing festival activities that often ran at a loss and required overnight work from council members, we expanded department events such as an algorithm contest, a Halloween party, and Coming-of-Age Day activities.

    I also opened the student council's first SNS channel and introduced open chat as a notice and communication channel.
    Instead of relying only on class representatives to create separate announcement chat rooms, we created a clearer flow where students could check notices and ask questions more easily.
  challenges: |
    Serving as president made me realize how closely budget, events, announcements, and communication are connected.
    Saving money was not enough on its own, so I kept adjusting the operating direction to make sure those savings returned to students as better experiences.
  impact: |
    This was where I used three years of student council and representative experience to actively solve problems from a position of responsibility.
    Questions about announcements decreased, and we were able to save student council funds while increasing the number of student events.
    It gave me confidence in noticing what a group needs and changing the flow into a better structure.
