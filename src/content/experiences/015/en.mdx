community:
  overview: |
    This was a campus startup club activity operated by the Inje University LINC+ Project Group.

    The team consisted of 2 designers and 3 to 4 developers, and our goal was to create a simple community app prototype. Rather than launching a polished service, the main purpose was to practice planning, communicating, and aligning direction across different backgrounds.
  motivation: |
    I agreed with a senior's opinion that building only with engineering students could be limiting when creating an actual service. Until then, most of my projects had been with friends from the same department, so I felt I needed experience working with non-developer roles.

    Because the club required presentations and progress reports, I was recommended to take the team lead role based on my strength in communication and presentation.
  role: |
    As the team lead, I coordinated planning meetings and the prototype production process.

    Rather than deciding everything on my own, I focused on organizing meetings and helping designers and developers align on the terms, criteria, and direction they needed to share. I also handled meeting notes, documentation, reports to the project group, and team announcements.
  contribution: |
    In the early planning meetings, it was difficult to align because each role used different terminology and decision criteria. I tried to make the idea easier to share by organizing it into a concrete prototype instead of leaving it only as verbal discussion.

    Through the support program, I also attended several startup lectures. Those sessions helped me look at the idea from a service perspective and reduced some of the gaps between design and development.
  challenges: |
    The hardest part was the early planning and design stage.

    Our main goal was to fill each other's gaps in experience, but each role had different familiar terms and standards. More than deciding the features themselves, we needed to make sure we were using the same words with the same meaning.
  impact: |
    This was my first experience seriously thinking about how to communicate with people who are not developers.

    After this activity, I began to pay more attention not only to implementation, but also to the background and language of the people I work with.
