community:
  overview: |
    This was a class representative role for about 70 first-year students in the Department of Computer Engineering in 2016.
    I organized the flow of announcements and questions between the department office, professors, and classmates.
  motivation: |
    I thought it would be the fastest way to understand what was happening in a new environment.

    I also wanted to communicate closely with other class representatives and the department office, then pass useful information to my classmates clearly.
  role: |
    I volunteered to become the first-year representative.
    I represented about 70 first-year students and acted as a communication point between classmates, the department office, and professors.
  contribution: |
    Most of my work involved sharing announcements and responding to questions.

    I tried to notice announcements early, ask again when something was unclear, and only share information once it was clear enough for others to act on.

    When the same question came up three or more times, I summarized it anonymously and shared the answer with everyone. For questions that were likely to repeat, such as location, process, or timing, I added the clarification before the first announcement.
  challenges: |
    I usually posted announcements once a day. During busy periods, I shared updates two or three times a day, and sometimes even on weekends during exam season.
    Because the class had about 70 first-year students, questions sometimes came in all at once, and it was not easy to organize the same information in a way everyone could understand.
  impact: |
    The department office told me that, compared with previous first-year groups, our class asked fewer repeated questions and that materials like exam schedules were organized clearly.

    My classmates also often said they felt reassured. Through this role, I learned to think about how information would be received, not just how it would be sent.

    It taught me to go beyond collecting information and organize it with the audience's context in mind.
