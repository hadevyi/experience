eventOperation:
  overview: |
    The 1st SSAFYnity Seminar was a career and technology seminar held on November 25, 2023.
    It invited senior industry experts and was open to both alumni and non-alumni participants.
  background: |
    The event was designed to help SSAFYnity grow beyond alumni networking into a wider IT network.
    It was also a step toward building a sustainable operating model for an independent nonprofit community.
  role: |
    As planning director of the 2nd SSAFYnity executive team, I planned the overall event, including speaker coordination, session structure, two-part operations, and post-event gathering matching.
    I served as opening MC, introducing SSAFYnity, giving the opening greeting, and facilitating the ice-breaking quiz.
  operation: |
    The seminar was split into two sessions due to capacity limits and included pre-submitted questions and post-event gathering matching.
  collaboration: |
    I worked with operators, speakers, and stakeholders to coordinate the seminar flow and participant guidance.
    Since each space required snacks, guidance, and operational support at the same time, aligning the operators' understanding of the flow was important.
  challenge: |
    Since the event was public, registration, fee, and guidance rules had to be clearer than internal alumni events.
    Running two repeated sessions across multiple spaces made operator alignment and time management difficult.
  result: |
    The event recorded 98 total participants and became an example of SSAFYnity operating a public career and technology seminar.
  learning: |
    Public seminars require clear registration rules, fee policies, guidance messages, and time management.
    When multiple sessions run in parallel, participant guidance and the opening atmosphere become important parts of event operations.
